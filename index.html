General information

Alexandria is an Egyptian city, founded in the fourth century BC, and it was called the bride of the Mediterranean. Alexandria is located west of Cairo, specifically on the Mediterranean Sea along the western edge of the Nile Delta

Alexandria climate

The city's climate is mild, with relatively hot summers and moderately cold winters. Summer starts from May to October, and temperatures range from 30-32 degrees Celsius, while in winter the weather is relatively humid, starting from November and extending until April, and temperatures range from 20 degrees Celsius during the day to 10 degrees Celsius at night and the winter season sees intermittent rain.

Land Marks

Qaitbay Citadel

About it

One of the oldest Islamic monuments and the most important tourist attractions in Alexandria, which receives large numbers of tourists throughout the year. This castle was built by Sultan “Ashraf Seif al-Din Qaytbay” on the Mediterranean coast in the same place as the old minaret, which was destroyed during an ancient strong earthquake; To be one of the impenetrable fortresses of the people and the army from the many threats that were inflicting on the city.

Entry ticket prices to the Qaitbay Citadel:

  • 20 EGP (for Egyptians and Arabs)
  • 30 EGP (for other nationalities)

Kom El Shoqafa tombs

About it

They are ancient tombs located in the Kom El Shoqafa area in Alexandria. They represent many ancient cultures. They are a mixture of Pharaonic arts with Roman and Greek arts, and contain some artifacts and many tombs and statues. The construction of these tombs has been expanded over the ages due to the presence of great differences in the decoration and inscriptions specific to each region.

Entry ticket prices to the Kom El Shoqafa cemetery:

  • 20 EGP (for Egyptians and Arabs)
  • 80 EGP (for other nationalities)
